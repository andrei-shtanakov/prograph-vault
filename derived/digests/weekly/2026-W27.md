## Key trends of the week

**Assessment:** Intensification of an existing trend, risen to a new level of maturity — from slogan to budget discipline in hardware and APIs.
**Forecast:** In the next 2–4 weeks, expect rising demand for model-routing and caching layers, plus new "cheap agents" from Google/OpenAI in response to Sonnet 5. Price pressure from DeepSeek/GLM will force closed APIs to cut promo prices again by the end of July.

### 2. Compute diversification: cracks in the NVIDIA (training), TSMC, and HBM monopolies

For the first time this cycle, several independent events at once strike three monopolies simultaneously: NVIDIA in training, TSMC on leading-edge nodes, the Samsung/SK Hynix duopoly on HBM. This isn't one SoTA but a systemic risk reduction for everyone building on GPUs.

**Supporting events:**

- [07.01 AI] — Qualcomm buys Modular (Mojo/MAX) for ~$3.92 billion: a software wedge against the CUDA moat at the toolchain level.
- [07.02 IT] — Qualcomm HBC: near-memory on LPDDR instead of scarce HBM, a claim of 6x bandwidth-per-watt on inference.
- [07.04 IT] — Anthropic is in talks with Samsung about its own 2nm inference ASIC — a signal that the Taiwanese monopoly on nodes has cracked (the web adds: in parallel, inference on Microsoft Maia 200 and revived Qualcomm–Tenstorrent talks worth $8–10 billion are being discussed).
- [07.04 IT] — Nvidia launches "compute now, pay later" (revenue-share instead of prepayment) — turning from a chip seller into a financier, which reads as the first hint of a demand ceiling.

**Assessment:** A new trend — previously these were isolated experiments, now a parallel front against three monopolies at once.
**Forecast:** In the coming month — more custom-silicon announcements from labs (Anthropic, OpenAI) and rising interest in AMD ROCm/Mojo as a portable layer. Nvidia's vendor-financing is worth monitoring as an overheating indicator.

### 3. AI ↔ cybersecurity: two-way escalation

The same engine — an LLM agent — this week showed itself both as the best bug hunter in history and as a new carrier of infection. This is a qualitative shift in the economics of security, not an isolated news item.

**Supporting events:**

**Forecast:** Scanning by agents will become part of CI at early-adopter teams as soon as July; expect the first commercial "agent-SAST" products and a reorientation of triage from severity to exploitability. AI-assistant configs and MCP servers will start being reviewed as executable artifacts.

## Cross-analysis: AI ↔ IT

The week's main pairing — how the physics of data centers began to dictate the economics of models. The AI-coding market ($9.3 billion, Claude Code ~40%) and cheap agents like Sonnet 5 generate such a volume of inference that GitHub hit Azure exhaustion and moved to AWS, its competitor, while the CPU:GPU ratio in inference drifted toward 1:1 with a Xeon shortage for all of 2026. That is, cheapening the token at the API level (DeepSeek, Sonnet 5, GLM) doesn't reduce total load but shifts the bottleneck from GPUs onto host CPUs, memory, and electricity. The engineering takeaway: when planning a cluster, CPUs and HBM are now counted as scarce resources on par with accelerators, and are budgeted first.

The second pairing — security as the flip side of agentification. The updated Claude Agent SDK (persistent REPL, MCP tunnels, self-hosted sandboxes) and Mythos, finding 29-year-old bugs, show the agent as a powerful defense tool; in the very same week the Miasma worm turns the configs of those same agents (.claude/.cursor) into an infection channel, while Five Eyes and CISA (a 3-day patching deadline) move this into the national-security plane. The same primitive — an executable agent context — simultaneously expands both the attack surface and the defense surface.

The third pairing — model commoditization pushes the moat into hardware and capital. While GLM-5.2 (MIT), Leanstral, and Sonnet 5 devalue "just a smart model," value shifts to those who own compute (OpenAI $122B, IREN–Mirantis, Nvidia-financier) or the tools of access to it (Qualcomm–Modular against CUDA, Zyphra on AMD, Anthropic on Samsung 2nm). Hardware diversification (trend 2) and the wave of M&A/financing (trend 5) are two sides of one process: when the model cheapens, the fight moves to the infrastructure beneath it.
